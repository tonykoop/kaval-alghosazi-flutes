# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A long slender wooden fipple flute presented as a finished physical instrument, laid at a clean 3/4 product-photography angle on a neutral light-gray studio sweep. It is a turned cylindrical open-pipe woodwind roughly two and a half feet long, lathe-rounded with a smooth satin oil finish that lets the warm grain of a pale European hardwood (elder or maple) show through. The recognizable feature is a fujara-style fipple head at the top end: the player blows into an end mouth inlet, and a side true sound window with a crisp splitting edge is cut into the body a short way down from the cap, the signature mark of this duct-fipple design. A short row of round tone holes runs along the front of the lower body. The bore is a single clean cylinder of even diameter. Soft directional studio light rakes from upper left, modeling the cylindrical surface and casting a soft shadow. ~50mm lens, shallow but controlled depth of field, photorealistic, high detail, no text labels, no maker marks.

## In-Context Prompt

The kaval and Alghosazi fipple flutes staged on a maker's workbench in a small woodworking shop, soft natural window light from the side, with curls of wood shaving, a few hand tools, and a length of split-blank stock blurred in the background. One flute rests on a felt-lined cradle while a second leans against a vise; the warm satin-oiled hardwood bodies glow against the muted shop tones. Environmental storytelling of a one-off prototype build, shallow depth of field, concept visualization only, photorealistic, no text or signage.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the fujara-style fipple head: the end mouth inlet at the very top, the turned cap and internal windway implied, and just below it on the side of the body the rectangular true sound window with its sharp splitting edge where the air column is excited. Raking low-angle light grazes across the window opening and splitting edge to reveal the crisp cut and the fine grain and satin finish of the pale hardwood. Extremely high detail on the wood texture and the clean edge geometry, no measurements, no callout lines, no text.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts, leader lines, or measurement numbers; no impossible or fantasy mechanisms, no metal keywork (these are open-hole flutes); anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
